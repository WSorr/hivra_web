System logic

Three pillars keep the network coherent.

Hivra treats value exchange as infrastructure. The system needs roles, relationship rules, and enough local autonomy to survive growth.

Value is modular

Starters work like capability tokens. They are not generic points; each one unlocks a different kind of contribution, incentive, or permission.

Relationships are structural

Capsules do not just transact. They build trust edges that determine how resources move, how roles propagate, and where the network can expand next.

Architecture stays distributed

No central server has to decide membership or meaning. The model is intended to preserve local control while still producing shared coordination.

Starter system

Five starters, five different signals.

Each starter is a distinct unit inside the capsule model, designed to encode a specific role in activation, continuity, and exchange.

J

Juice

Energy for initial activation. Juice bootstraps motion and helps a capsule become operational.

S

Spark

Impulse for catalytic actions. Spark marks moments that trigger new pathways or relationships.

S

Seed

Continuity signal for replication and growth. Seed carries forward the logic of the system into new nodes.

P

Pulse

Rhythm and synchronization across the network. Pulse supports timing, cadence, and ongoing alignment.

K

Kick

Directed push for decisive change. Kick is the force behind transitions, escalation, and boundary crossing.
